George Pérez

Comic Book Writer, Artist, Legend!

Illustration of George Pérez with The New Teen Titans - the subject of one of his most memorable comic runs - done by fellow comic book artist José Luis García-López for the DC Comics tribute following Pérez's death.

A Lifetime through Infinite Events

  • June 9 1954 - Born in South Bronx, New York
  • 1959/1960 - Began Drawing with an aspiration to become an artist
  • 1973 - Became artist Rich Buckner's assistant (first professional work in the comic industry)
  • August 1974 - Made his professional debut in comics pencilling a 2 page story in Marvel Comics' Astonishing Tales #25
  • August 1975 - Began his rise to prominence at Marvel. Began doing the art for The Avengers #141
  • December 1975 - Along with writer Bill Mantlo, he co-created the first Puerto Rican superhero in Comics - The White Tiger
  • 1979 - Had his first significant collaboration with future frequent collaborator, writer Marv Wolfman, on Fantastic Four Annual #14
  • May 1980 - Along with writer David Micheline he co-created villain - Taskmaster
  • September 1980 - Began working for DC Comics with The Flash #289
  • November 1980 - Began first major long-term collaboration with Marv Wolfman, The New Teen Titans. The run would become heralded as one of his best comic book runs. In the process they created characters such as Cyborg, Raven, Starfire, and the "Changeling" incarnation of Beast Boy.
  • 1985 - Took leave from The New Teen Titans and began working, again with Marv Wolfman, on DC's 50th Anniversary event, Crisis on Infinite Earths. This would go on to become one of the most influential and well known runs in comics history.
  • 1987 - Assisted with a major reboot of the Wonder Woman Character - worked as both artist and co-writer.
  • 1988 - Became the main writer on Wonder Woman
  • December 1988 - Rejoined the creative team of "New Teen Titans" which was renamed to The New Titans
  • 1991 - Departed DC Comics after numerous issues with editorial.
  • February 1998 - After spending most of the 90s out of the spotlight, he returned to The Avengers.
  • Late 2000 - Co-founded Hero Initiative a charity of comic creators aimed at "creating a financial safety net for comic creators who may need emergency medical aid, financial support for life essentials, and an avenue back to paying work".
  • 2003 - Did the art for a DC/Marvel crossover event - JLA/Avengers.
  • May 2006 - Returned to DC Comics working primarily as a guest artist on various books.
  • Early 2012 - Departed DC Comics once again due to continued issues with editorial.
  • January 2019 - Announced his retirement from Comics due to various health issues. Said he would continue making convention appearances on a "very limited basis".
  • December 2021 - Was diagnosed with "inoperable pancreatic cancer".
  • May 6 2022 - Passed away due to complications from cancer at the age of 67.

"George Pérez made everything look effortless...George’s stories were a joy to read, and his work resonated with everyone he met."

-- DC Comics Twitter Account
