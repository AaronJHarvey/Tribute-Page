George Pérez

Comic Book Writer, Artist, Legend!

Illustration of George Pérez with The New Teen Titans - the subject of one of his most memorable comic runs - done by fellow comic book artist José Luis García-López for the DC Comics tribute following Pérez's death.

A Lifetime through Infinite Events

June 9, 1954

Born in South Bronx, New York

Began Drawing with an aspirations of becoming an artist

1959/1960
1973

Became artist Rich Buckner's assistant (first professional work in the comic industry)

Made his professional debut in comics pencilling a 2 page story in Marvel Comics' Astonishing Tales #25

August 1974
December 1975

Along with writer Bill Mantlo, he co-created the first Puerto Rican superhero in Comics - The White Tiger

Had his first significant collaboration with future frequent collaborator, writer Marv Wolfman, on Fantastic Four Annual #14

1979
May 1980

Along with writer David Micheline he co-created villain - Taskmaster

Began working for DC Comics with The Flash #289

September 1980
November 1980

Began first major long-term collaboration with Marv Wolfman, The New Teen Titans. The run would become heralded as one of his best comic book runs. In the process they created characters such as Cyborg, Raven, Starfire, and the "Changeling" incarnation of Beast Boy.

Took leave from The New Teen Titans and began working, again with Marv Wolfman, on DC's 50th Anniversary event, Crisis on Infinite Earths. This would go on to become one of the most influential and well known runs in comics history.

1985
1987

Assisted with a major reboot of the Wonder Woman Character - worked as both artist and co-writer.

Became the main writer on Wonder Woman

1988
December 1988

Rejoined the creative team of "New Teen Titans" which was renamed to The New Titans

Departed DC Comics after numerous issues with editorial.

1991
February 1998

After spending most of the 90s out of the spotlight, he returned to The Avengers

Co-founded Hero Initiative a charity of comic creators aimed at "creating a financial safety net for comic creators who may need emergency medical aid, financial support for life essentials, and an avenue back to paying work".

Late 2000
2003

Did the art for a DC/Marvel crossover event - JLA/Avengers.

Returned to DC Comics working primarily as a guest artist on various books.

May 2006
Early 2012

Departed DC Comics once again due to continued issues with editorial.

Announced his retirement from Comics due to various health issues. Said he would continue making convention appearances on a "very limited basis".

January 2019
December 2021

Was diagnosed with "inoperable pancreatic cancer".

Passed away due to complications from cancer at the age of 67.

May 6, 2022

"George Pérez made everything look effortless...George’s stories were a joy to read, and his work resonated with everyone he met."

-- DC Comics Twitter Account
